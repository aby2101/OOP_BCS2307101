How to Perform Solah (Prayer) in Islam

Solah (prayer) is one of the Five Pillars of Islam, performed five times a day by Muslims. This tutorial will guide you through the steps of performing Solah.

Steps to Perform Solah

  • Step 1: Make the intention (niyyah) to perform prayer.
  • Step 2: Perform Wudu (ablution) to cleanse yourself.
  • Step 3: Stand facing the Qibla (direction of the Kaaba in Mecca).
  • Step 4: Start by saying "Allahu Akbar" (Allah is the Greatest).
  • Step 5: Recite Surah Al-Fatiha (the Opening) followed by another Surah from the Quran.
  • Step 6: Perform Ruku (bowing), keeping your back and head level, and say "Subhana Rabbiyal Azim" (Glory is to my Lord, the Almighty).
  • Step 7: Stand up straight again and say "Sami' Allahu liman hamidah, Rabbana lakal hamd" (Allah hears the one who praises Him, our Lord, to You is due all praise).
  • Step 8: Perform Sujood (prostration), placing your forehead, nose, palms, knees, and toes on the ground, saying "Subhana Rabbiyal A'la" (Glory is to my Lord, the Most High).
  • Step 9: Sit up briefly between the two Sujood (prostrations) and say "Rabbighfir li" (My Lord, forgive me).
  • Step 10: Repeat Sujood and complete the cycle for each Rak'ah (unit of prayer).
  • Step 11: At the end of the prayer, say the Tasleem (salutation) to the right and left: "Assalamu Alaikum wa Rahmatullah" (Peace and mercy of Allah be upon you).

Conclusion

Solah is a crucial act of worship for Muslims and is performed at specific times throughout the day. The tutorial above covers the basic steps involved in performing Solah. Consistent practice will help you perfect your prayer and strengthen your connection with Allah.
